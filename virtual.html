OUR FAVORITE HOME RECIPES

SURYA'S LEMON PASTA

This recipe was modified from one given to us by Alex's gram. Since it's so delicious and quick to put together, it's quickly become one of our weekly staples.

MAKES 4 SERVINGS
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • ⅓ cup heavy cream
  • ⅓ cup olive oil
  • ⅓ cup (30 grams) of grated parmigiano reggiano
  • ¼ cup fresh lemon juice, plus zest of 1-2 lemons
  • Handful of chopped/chiffonaded basil
STEPS
  1. Bring pot of aggressively-salted water to a boil and begin to cook the pasta
  2. As the pasta is cooking, combine the heavy cream, olive oil, cheese, and lemon zest/juice in a heat-proof mixing bowl. Thoroughly whisk together until a cohesive sauce forms
  3. When the pasta is done, use tongs to directly move the spagetti from the pot to the mixing bowl - this will carry some pasta water with it that will help it come together. Toss and coat.
  4. Serve and garnish with the basil - don't skip this step! It really brings everything together.

SURYA'S CARBONARA

Surya first picked up this recipe during his summer stint in the middle of Italy, and has made small modifications over time. Definitely more rustic than your traditional carbonara!

MAKES 4 SERVINGS
INGREDIENTS
  • 5 strips of bacon (for vegetarians, substitute with 1 cup of sliced shitake mushrooms)
  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 3 eggs (for more rich flavor, use 2 eggs and 2 egg yolks)
  • Enough parmigiano reggiano to make a thick sauce (~½ cup)
  • 1 small shallot, chopped finely
  • 1/3 cup green peas
STEPS
  1. Start by prepping the sauce - whisk together the eggs and cheese to make a sauce. Season with black pepper (no salt)
  2. Begin to cook the pasta
  3. As the pasta is cooking, start to fry the bacon in another pan. If substituting mushrooms, add 2 tbps of olive oil and add the shallots immediately
  4. Once the bacon is done, remove onto a paper towel and drain all but 2 tbsp of grease. Fry shallots for 4 minutes
  5. In the last minute of cooking pasta, add the green peas and ¼ cup of pasta water to the shallot pan and whisk
  6. Once the pasta is done cooking, time to move quickly. Use tongs to move the pasta into the pan, kill the heat, toss with the fat, shallots, and peas. Then, slowly add the cheese sauce over the pasta and rapidly toss to incorporate. If the sauce looks too dry, add another ¼ cup of pasta water
ALEX'S OATMEAL CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES

Alex claims this is the best chocolate chip cookie in the world. Definitely no arguments from Surya. If you want to make everyone in the world your friend, make these cookies!

MAKES 20 COOKIES
INGREDIENTS
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups oatmeal
  • ½ cup shortening
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 1 tbps molasses
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ⅔ cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips
STEPS
  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)
  2. In a large bowl, cream shortening and sugars until fluffy
  3. Beat in egg, stir in molasses and vanilla extract
  4. In a medium bowl, combine flour, oats, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt
  5. Gradually add to creamed mixture
  6. Stir in chocolate chips
  7. Roll into 1-1/2 in balls, 2 in apart on greased baking sheet
  8. Bake for 8-10 min, until golden brown
  9. Cool for 5-10 minutes before removing from wire, cookies will be gooey and will need time to firm
ALEX'S FRUIT OF THE FARM PIE

In Alex's first ever pie-baking competition, she won 3rd place with this pie against some serious heavy weights. She claims it was the worst one she's ever made! It's just that good

MAKES...well...one pie
Pie Crust
INGREDIENTS
  • 2 ½ cups all purpose flour
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 7 tbsp unsalted butter (chilled and cubed)
  • ¾ cup vegetable shortening (chilled)
  • ½ cup ice water
STEPS
  1. Mix flour & salt in large bowl. Add butter/shortening chunks.
  2. With a pastry cutter or two forks, cut the butter/shortening into the mixture until small pea-sized bits appear and mixture has course, consistent texture.
  3. Slowly drizzle and stir approximately 1/2 cup of ice water until flour comes together. Do not add any more water than necessary.
  4. Work dough until constant, divide into two discs, Sara wrap and place in refrigerator for at least 3 hours or at most 5 days.
Pie Filling
INGREDIENTS
  • 3 cups Granny Smith Apples
  • 2 cups strawberries
  • 1 cup blackberries
  • ⅓ cup light brown sugar
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • ⅓ cup cornstarch
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • ¼ tbsp salt
  • 1 egg beaten + splash of milk
STEPS
  1. Preheat oven to 400 F
  2. Stir filling together in large bowl. Taste and play with extra lemon or sugar depending on tartness of batch of apples and strawberries
  3. Add filling to rolled pie crust, leave out excess liquid
  4. Cut top pie crust into strips and weave into lattice
  5. Brush top of crust with Egg and milk mixture and sprinkle turbinaro sugar on top as garnish
  6. Bake for 25 min at 400, then lower heat to 350 and back for 30-35 min, add a pie cover for the edges if they brown too much at 400.
  7. Allow pie to cool for 3 hours before serving. Alex enjoys it served cold.

OUR FAVORITE TOASTS

SURYA'S OLD FASHIONED

Surya's record for making one of these is 20 seconds. He spilled half the drink though, so does it count?

INGREDIENTS
  • 2 oz bourbon (Surya's preferred: Russell's Reserve)
  • 0.75 oz simple syrup
  • 2-3 dashes of angostura bitters (or Regan's orange bitters if like Surya, you can't stand Angostura)
  • lots of ice

Put it all in a glass and stir for 20 seconds

SURYA'S KENTUCKY MULE

What happens when you hate vodka but love drinking a cold drink out of a Moscow Mule cup!

INGREDIENTS
  • 2 oz bourbon (Surya's preferred: Four Roses Small Batch)
  • ¾ oz fresh lime juice
  • 5-10 mint leaves, plus 1 stem for garnish
  • ginger beer (Surya's preferred: Fever Tree)
  • crushed ice

Muddle the mint leaves in the bottom of a shaker. Add bourbon and lime juice, then shake for 40 seconds. Double strain into Moscow Mule cup filled with crushed ice. Top with ginger beer to taste and a mint garnish. Optionally use a metal straw!

ALEX'S ITALIAN 75

Basically a French 75, but with Prosecco! Champagne is too stuffy anyways...

INGREDIENTS
  • 2 oz gin
  • ¾ oz fresh lemon juice
  • 0.75 oz simple syrup
  • 3 oz prosecco

Shake the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup over ice for 40 seconds. Double strain into glass and top with prosecco. Garnish with lemon or orange twist - optionally float one barspoon of Aperol on top.

ALEX'S ROSEMARY MAPLE BOURBON SOUR

Alex's all-time favorite cocktail, and the only bourbon cocktail she likes! A great fall drink we discovered when we first moved to Chicago.

INGREDIENTS
  • 2 oz bourbon (Alex's preferred: Knob Creek)
  • 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 0.75 oz maple syrup
  • 1 large sprig of rosemary

Muddle the rosemary at the bottom of the shaker. Add bourbon, lemon juice, maple syrup, and ice. Shake for 30 seconds. Serve with a rosemary garnish.
